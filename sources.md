# Data Sources & Methodology

## Air Quality
- **PM2.5 / PM10 / SO2 concentration data (2018–2022):** Department of Statistics Malaysia (DOSM) Open Data, "Air Pollution" dataset. https://open.dosm.gov.my/data-catalogue/air_pollution
  Monthly average concentrations, derived from hourly observations at monitoring stations nationwide.
- **WHO Annual PM2.5 Guideline (5 µg/m³):** WHO Global Air Quality Guidelines, 2021.
- **Malaysia SO2 Regulatory Standard (0.04 ppm, 24-hr):** Department of Environment Malaysia, Air Pollutant Index of Malaysia (APIMS).

## Energy Transition
- **Installed capacity mix, 2021 (22.6% RE):** Suruhanjaya Tenaga (Energy Commission), Malaysia Energy Statistics Handbook 2023 (capacity data dated 31 December 2021). https://www.st.gov.my
- **2025 RE capacity achievement (32%, 14.86GW):** SEDA Malaysia, reported July 2026. https://thesun.my/business/malaysias-renewables-capacity-reaches-14-86gw/
- **NETR targets (31% by 2025, 40% by 2035, 70% by 2050; coal phase-out by 2044):** National Energy Transition Roadmap, Ministry of Economy, 2023. https://ekonomi.gov.my/sites/default/files/2023-08/National%20Energy%20Transition%20Roadmap.pdf
- **RE reporting accountability gap:** Demmel, S. (2025). "Malaysia's Renewable Energy Target Jungle: Are Unclear Definitions and Insufficient Monitoring Reducing Accountability?" Asia School of Business. https://asb.edu.my/wp-content/uploads/2025/01/ASB-Working-Paper_Malaysia-Decarbonisation.pdf

## Planetary Health Policy
- **National Planetary Health Action Plan (NPHAP):** Academy of Sciences Malaysia (ASM) / MOSTI, launched November 2025. Six Key Result Areas: Governance; Environment and Health; Sustainable Food; Research and Education; Energy Transition; Values, Cultural Shift, Behavioural Change and Communication.
- **National Environmental Health Action Plan (NEHAP), 2013, and 2016–2020 implementation review:** Ministry of Health Malaysia. http://nehapmalaysia.moh.gov.my

## Stakeholder Documentation
- **Ministry of Health (MOH):** NEHAP (2013) and its documented 2016–2020 implementation gap; ongoing SCPH–MOH–Health Care Without Harm collaboration on healthcare facility decarbonisation.
- **PETRA (Ministry of Energy Transition & Water Transformation):** NETR (2023), as above.
- **Malaysian Medical Association (MMA):** The Lancet Countdown on Health and Climate Change: Policy Brief for Malaysia, Sunway University, 2022. https://sunwayuniversity.edu.my/sites/default/files/sustainability/Lancet%20Countdown%202022%20-%20Malaysia%20Policy%20Brief_EN.pdf
- **Tenaga Nasional Berhad (TNB):** TNB Sustainability Report 2024. https://www.tnb.com.my/assets/annual_report/TNB_Sustainability_Report_2024.pdf
- **Klima Action Malaysia (KAMY):** "Navigating the Roadmap: An Examination of Malaysia's NETR," 2023. https://medium.com/klimaaction/navigating-the-roadmap-an-examination-of-malaysias-netr-3451bed09730

## Stakeholder Scoring Methodology
Influence Level is scored on three criteria: regulatory/legal authority, resource or infrastructure control, and public reach/legitimacy. Alignment is scored on stated public position, documented track record, and any countervailing structural interest. Priority Score = Influence × Alignment. Engagement strategy per institution follows directly from its position on the Influence × Alignment grid, adapted from Mendelow, A. (1991), the Power-Interest stakeholder matrix.
